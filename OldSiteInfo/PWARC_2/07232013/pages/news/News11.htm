Bill, WA0KAQ, reported that the club did very well in its first ARRL Field Day exercise last June. He gave the following statistics that are also listed on the club's website:

    In competition with all Colorado stations, regardless of class, the club placed 10th out of 57 entries. This put the club in the top 18% of the entries.

    In competition with all stations regardless of class in the Rocky Mountain Division (CO, NM, UT, WY), the club placed 21st out of 116 entries. This also put the club in the top 18% of the entries.

    Nationwide, against all classes, the club placed 513th out of 2,617 entries. This placed the club in the top 20% of the entries.

    Nationwide, competing against all stations with a multiplier of 5 for their score, the club placed 57th out of 276 entries. The placed the club in the top 21%.

    Nationwide, competing against all stations running in our 2AB class, the club placed 7th of 16 entries ( top 44%).

    Full results have been published in the December QST.

    Bill noted that one thing that kept the club from placing even higher was that only one station ran through the whole night due to the lack of a 75-meter antenna. He suggested that the club have an antenna party in the spring and build a 75-meter dipole and any other antennas it feels may be necessary.
