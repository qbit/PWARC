ARRL Membership Report:

The club currently has 33 members, with 31 voting (licensed) members. The club now has members holding 9 Technician licenses, 7 General licenses, 2 Advanced licenses, and 13 Amateur Extra licenses. Of the 31 voting members, 20 (64.5%) belong to the ARRL.

New ARRL Memberships or Renewals: None

News from the members: (What have you done in the last month that pertains to amateur radio?)

  • Neal, KD0MBL, helped Stan, KC0OYO, put up a Rohn 25G tower and beam. He also made a fox hunt transmitter using his handheld with VOX and an MP3 player and hid it during the May 14 fox hunt.

  • Stan, KC0OYO, is enjoying his new tower and Mosley mini-beam.

  • New member Jim, N0OBV, is having considerable interference from a nearby power transformer on HF. He is working with the power company to resolve it. He is becoming active once again after some time away from ham radio. While he waits for the interference issue to be settled, he has been working on his test equipment.

  • New member Amanda, K1DDN, has been very active on 40 meters where she calls a new one night a week. An outline for an article she is writing has been accepted by CQ Magazine.

  • Billy, K5LKT, used his 40 meter dipole on 6 meters to contact Idaho.

  • New member Mason, WA6HSI, has been off the air for 25 years and is thinking about buying a more modern rig so he can be active on HF.

  • Jim, NE0DA, helped install Stan's tower.

  • Bill, KD0ICM, recently returned from a trip to Florida and has not been on the air.

  • Bill, W0NHF, has his Yaesu FT-897D back from Vertex. He reported he has been working some stations on 10 meter CW.

  • Paul, KB0JIT, recently received a QSL for a contact with the Queen Mary. He has also been working on a Net Log software program.

  • Dan, NG0T, has not been active due to his tax business.

  • David, KY6YYY, worked stations in Mexico, Canada and Lithuania. He now has about 25 states worked as well. He also participated in the club's fox hunt.

  • Jane, KA0USA, was one of the three "hounds" in the club's fox hunt. She recently received a special event certificate from Buckingham Air Field in Florida, and she has been NCS for the club's VHF net.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, was more active than usual during the last month. He helped install Stan's tower, hid a fox, and drove Jane around during the fox hunt. QSL cards arrived from the Isle of Man and Surinam. DX entities he worked include the Canary Islands, Hawaii, Macedonia, Finland, Serbia and French Guiana on RTTY, and Hawaii and Hungary on PSK31. Three special event stations also made it into his log. He also made long distance contacts on 2 meters, contacting the International Space Station on two passes using APRS. On May 5, an opening on 6 meters provided contacts wieth CO, AL, AR, LA, TN and TX. He also helped Clark program Clark's FT-7900 mobile radio.

  • Guest Clark Clendenen is studying for his Technician license and will take the test in June. He recently purchased a Yaesu FT-7900 duo-band mobile rig and a Yaesu FT-60 duo-band handheld and is getting them set up so he can listen to 2 meters and 70 cm.

NA0PW Logging Program

  • At the May meeting Ted, K0LDS, volunteered to be the club's QSL Manager and has installed the club's new logging program on his computer. All logs are up to date, and a back-up copy is held by WA0KAQ. Both individuals can also access the eQSL website and manage confirmations there. Logs in .adi format can be e-mailed to him to be dropped into the ACLog database. Microsoft Exel files in the appropriate format can also be converted for our use.
