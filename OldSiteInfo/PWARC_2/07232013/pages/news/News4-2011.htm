News from the members: (What have you done in the last month that pertains to amateur radio?)

  • Bill, W0NHF, has sent his Yaesu FT-897D to Vertex for repair. He is currently getting reacquainted with a QRP rig he built many years ago and is working CW with it on 20 meters.

  • Paul, KB0JIT, recently helped David, KY6YYY tune a fan dipole for 40-10 meters. He also helped Marty, WA0YNE (non-member) from Pueblo take down an antenna. Marty will be moving to Denver.

  • Stanley, WB0JRG, helped take down WA0YNE's antenna.

  • Neal, KD0MBL, helped Marty take down his antenna. He also has remounted his screwdriver antenna in his back yard and worked on making a fox hunt transmitter using his handheld with VOX and an MP3 player.

  • Bill, KB0RPD, was active with the American Red Cross during the wildfire in Bent County.

  • David, KY6YYY, has been enjoying his HF privileges on 20 meters while he waits for his antenna tuner to be repaired. He reported working VA, NJ, OH, FL, HI, NV and ID.

  • Ted, K0LDS, has been active on HF in the mornings. He recently worked Vietnam, which is a rare DX country. In addition to occasional weekend contesting, he recently purchased a small software defined radio kit designed by KB9YIG.

  • Jane, KA0USA, has been busy gathering donated items for the club's upcoming Yard Sale in June. She also recently worked Spain and the Slovak Republic and received a QSL from a contact with a lighthouse in Michigan.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, has been working on backup comm plans for the Liberty Point 5K Run on July 4 sponsored by United Way of Pueblo. He is also putting together information for the event's tactical net. He also reports he was more active on the air during the last month. He worked Tunisia on SSB for the first time, and reworked Cyprus after waiting three years for a confirmation from another station there. He also worked Australia, New Zealand, Belgium and the Dominican Republic on PSK31, Russia and Asiatic Russia on RTTY, and Papua New Guinea on CW. He received LOTW confirmations from Cyprus and St. Barthelemy. His DX total now stands as 161 countries worked and 153 confirmed.

  • Guest Amanda Alden, K1DDN, stated she has been very "radio active" since recently upgrading to General Class. She is NCS once a week for a net on 40 meters, has 43 states verified and is a "daily radio user."

  • Guest Mason Meek, WA6HSI, has not been active for 25 years, but the ham radio bug has bitten him once again. He has taken a Yaesu FT-101 transceiver out of mothballs and is working to repair it. The receiver is working, but the transmitter needs to be repaired.

  • Review: PWARC Net

    • For the past five weeks, the club has been calling the first session of the Tuesday night VHF net on the Royal Gorge Ham Club repeater at Canon City. The number of check-ins were as follows:

      • Week One: 20 on repeater, 2 additional on local simplex = 22

      • Week Two: 13 on repeater, no additional on local simplex = 13

      • Week Three: 13 on repeater, 1 additional on local simplex = 13

      • Week Four: 12 on repeater, 1 additional on local simplex = 13

      • Week Five: 11 on repeater, no additional on local simplex = 11

    • WA0KAQ said the period for the test was now over, and asked the members whether they wanted to continue using the repeater or revert to their simplex only net. Several members voiced their opinions that the repeater access was good for the net, and they enjoyed hearing stations check in from Canon City and the surrounding area. The club appreciates the Royal Gorge Ham Club letting us use their repeater, and the consensus was that we should continue to use it. The simplex net will also continue following the repeater net, as it encourages members to improve on their antennas and keep them in good condition. Members are encouraged to check in to BOTH nets if they have the necessary equipment.

New Business:

  • Electronic Logging Program

    • With the club participating in Field Day, the ARRL School Roundup and now a Special Event Station, WA0KAQ said the club needs to have some kind of standard for logging contacts made by the club station. After a discussion of some different logging programs, Stanley, WB0JRG, made a motion that the club purchase a copy of ACLog software by N3FJP. Paul, KB0JIT, seconded the motion. The cost of the program is $19.00. The program can import .adi logging files from our Field Day logging program (also from N3FPJ) or any other program using that popular file format.

    • WA0KAQ also asked for a volunteer to be the club's QSL Manager. The club's newest member, Ted, K0LDS, said he would be happy to provide this service. He will download the software, buy the $19 registration, and present the bill to the club. Logs in .adi format can be e-mailed to him to be dropped into the ACLog database.
