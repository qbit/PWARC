ARRL Membership Report:

The club has 33 members, 32 of whom are licensed. Of those 10 hold Technician licenses, 6 have General Licenses, 2 hold Advanced licenses, and 14 have Amateur Extra licenses. Of the 32 voting members, 22 belong to ARRL. (68.75%).

New ARRL Memberships or Renewals: We have three ARRL Membership Renewals this month: Paul (KB0JIT), Amanda (K1DDN), and Jeff (K0JSC)

News from the members: (What have you done in the last month pertaining to amateur radio?)

  • Bob, WD0FBW, reports he has repaired a Heathkit HW-8 four-band QRP CW transceiver.

  • Amanda, K1DDN, reported she did her usual HF nets and also helped the Royal Gorge Ham Club with the Run the Rim 5 and 10K runs on Oct. 1.

  • Ted, K0LDS, participated in the CQ World Wide CW Contest.

  • Mason, WA6HSI, reported that the wind has bent the mast for his Slim Jim 2-meter antenna. He will be working to repair it and is working on getting on 10 meters as well.

  •  Neal, KD0MBL, has his 2-meter antenna receiving better after repositioning it on the roof. He also put up a 40 meter Windom antenna. For 9 days he was camping in the mountains and found RMS Express very useful to send e-mail and text messages while having no cell phone or internet connection.

  • Ed, NI5R, is recovering nicely from heart surgery. He has been on the air very little in the last month.

  • Billy, K5LKT, had been out of the hospital for 5 days. He said he has a good 2-meter antenna up now and is also working 6-meter repeaters. For DX, he reports working N. Korea, Israel and Chatham Island. He also worked a station he had worked 20 years ago.

  • Paul, KB0JIT, reports little radio activity and was gone to Iowa for about a week. He helped with the VE Session earlier in the day.

  • Jim, NE0DA, is enjoying 10 meter openings to Europe and also worked a Special Event station in Maine. He enjoyed helping with communications for the Run the Rim event at Royal Gorge on Oct. 1.

  • Stan, KC0OYO, said he had worked some 2-meter stations during the last month but has spent most of his free time fishing at the reservoir.

  • David, KY6YYY, was in the hospital briefly to have a skin cancer removed from his head. He has been busy on HF working DX that includes Germany, France, Cuba and the T32C DXpedition to Eastern Kiribati.

  • Jane, KA0USA, now has back-up power for her rig. She received a Special Event QSL from N6R commemorating the 100th birthday of President Ronald Reagan. She also worked Ecuador on 10 meter SSB.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, made contacts on 12 meters using a 40 meter dipole that lies on the roof at one point. One contact was with Iceland on RTTY, and the other was with Belgium on PSK31. Using the same dipole, he worked Scotland on 15 meter PSK31, England on RTTY, and Special Event Station TM33LDX in France on RTTY. He had an interesting 10 meter PSK31 contact with Fukushima, Japan, which was devastated by the earthquake and tsunami earlier this year, and he also worked T77NM in San Marino on 10 meter SSB for a new country.

  1. Emergency Plan finalization (WA0KAQ)

  3. Constitutional Change

    • When our Constitution was first formed, it was set up to model one found on the Internet. This included elected officers for President, Vice-President, Secretary, Treasurer and Logistics/Operations. The Logistics/Operations office was established to serve in the case of an emergency situation. Now that the club has established an emergency plan, there is the question as to whether we need an elected Logistics/Operations Officer.

    • The duties of the Logistics/Operations officer have been assimilated into the club's new Emergency Plan

    • Under the expanded emergency/disaster plan the Officer in Charge has the option to appoint a temporary Logistics Officer if needed.

    • By eliminating Logistics/Operations as an elected position, it would also help to streamline the annual election process by eliminating the need to find someone to fill the position each year.

    • David (KY6YYY) made a motion to eliminate the position as an elected office, and Ed (NI5R) seconded the motion. The motion passed by a unanimous vote. The proposal would be circulated to all members for their comments and voted on at the next meeting. The measure would take a 2/3 majority of members present at the next meeting to become effective.
