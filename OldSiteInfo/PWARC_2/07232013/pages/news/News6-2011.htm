ARRL Membership Report:

    Following the meeting, our club now has 33 members, with 31 voting (licensed) members. The club now has members holding 9 Technician licenses, 7 General licenses, 2 Advanced licenses, and 13 Amateur Extra licenses. Of the 31 voting members, 20 (64.5%) belong to the ARRL.

New ARRL Memberships or Renewals: None

News from the members: (What have you done in the last month that pertains to amateur radio?)

  • Paul, KB0JIT, has been fine tuning a Butternut vertical for use at Field Day.

  • Neal, KD0MBL, set up the Special Event station at the library and worked several stations.

  • Lori, AH6OS, has been checking in on the net now that her outside antenna is working. Paul fixed a short in a coax connector for her. She also ordered a second battery for her handheld.

  • Ed, NI5R, helped with the Special Event station.

  • Billy, K5LKT, has been working some CW.

  • Jim, NE0DA, now has the rotor on his HF beam fixed and worked several Navy ships during the Museum Ships Special Event.

  • New member Clark Clendenen spent the month studying for his Technician license and passed his exam at the VE Session preceding our meeting. Stan, KC0OYO, built a 2-meter beam for him, and Clark has purchased a TV rotor for it. He has also ordered a General Class study guide and a new auxiliary speaker.

  • Bob, WD0FBW, said he has been checking out his vintage Heathkit gear to see if it still works. The power supply needs to have its capacitors replaced before he can check any farther.

  • David, KY6YYY, reported working Guatemala and California, and received QSL cards from Mexico and California. He also participated in the Yard Sale/Bake Sale and the club's Special Event.

  • Stan, KC0OYO, built a 2-meter beam for Clark from TV antenna parts.

  • Amanda, K1DDN, worked stations from home for the Special Event. She and Jeff will be in Missouri for Field Day.

  • Jane, KA0USA, worked 27 PSK31 contacts from home for the Special Event. She also headed the Yard Sale/Bake Sale for the club.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, was active again during the last month. He programmed handhelds for Lori and Clark and made "cheat sheets" to help them with the more basic functions. He worked PSK31 and SSB from home during the Special Event, replaced a memory battery in Jane's handheld, and rebuilt three battery packs for his handheld. QSL cards were received for an APRS contact with the International Space Station and from Papua, New Guinea. During a period of about 1-1/2 hours during the ARRL VHF Contest, he worked 24 stations in 9 states and Canada on a 6 meter SSB opening. He also worked Vermont and Texas through satellite AO-27 on June 15, his first satellite contacts in more than 15 years. On June 16 he worked New Caledonia on 20 meter PSK31.

Old Business:

  • Special Events Station - Pueblo Flood of June 3, 1921

    • A total of 234 stations were worked during the Special Event, and Ted, K0LDS, has the club's logging program up to date. He reported archiving 70 contacts from the event on eQSL, and WA0KAQ archived another 15 contacts this week. WA0KAQ reported that only about a dozen requests for certificates have been received. Amanda, K1DDN, will print 50 certificates for the club.

    • Members were asked to have their contacts listed using UTC for all future contacts, as there was some confusion with log times when inputting some of the logs into the main database this time. Information about UTC and a conversion chart have been sent to all members.

    • WA0KAQ reported working Special Event W0E recently, and the club had a website designed so that those with confirmed contacts could print their own certificates. He contacted the club in St. Louis, and they offered their services in setting up a free website for any special event that our club might have in the future. Those who worked our station would be able to print their certificates within minutes of working our station, saving time and money for all involved.

  • Field Day Committee report

    • Paul, KB0JIT, reported most things seem to be in place for the annual ARRL Field Day to be held June 25 & 26 at Pueblo County Fire Station #1 east of the library. He has been working on a Butternut vertical for the club to use, and he and Neal, KD0MBL, will have solar panels on site to charge batteries.

    • Kirk, KD0LDH, has donated two 18-AH batteries for back-up use, and Ed, NI5R, will charge them by solar power before the event.

    • Jim, NE0DA, volunteered the use of a G5RV dipole for use on bands down to 75 meters.

    • FD Chairman Bill Fortune, KB0JIT, is asking participants to bring chairs, bug spray and rain gear. Battery powered lights are needed for night work as well. He stated a few more batteries may be received from the NWS before the event.

    • The event has been listed on ARRL's Field Day Locator web page. David, KY6YYY, has posted flyers around Pueblo West, and Bill, WA0KAQ, has sent an article to the local newspaper and formal invitations to members of the PW Fire Department and PW Sheriff's office.

    • Baked goods not sold at the Bake Sale have been frozen and will be provided for snacks.

  • Liberty Point 5K Run - July 4, 2011

    • WA0KAQ reported he has been in contact with Bianca Hicks of United Way of Pueblo County. Those working the event have been sent a time schedule and the locations where they will be posted. Team members will be watching for anyone needing assistance along the route. In addition, some members will be acting as "Course Marshals" to keep people from removing orange cones from intersections and driving around them to get onto Purcell Blvd.

WA0KAQ will be NCS from near the south end of the run, just north of Liberty Point. All participants are asked to wear a blaze orange safety vest. A few vests may be available to lend to those who do not have one.

New Business:

  • Emergency Communications Plan

    • Bill, W0NHF, suggested on the June 14 simplex net that the club needs a written emergency communications plan. This was brought before the club by WA0KAQ.

    • After some discussion about what kind of plan was needed and how much support it would receive from members, Vice-president David Yeater, KY6YYY, suggested the club write a plan in steps. The first step would be to set up a plan that would address what members should do as a group should the power fail, telephones go out, etc. After this plan is written and put into action, the club could then implement steps for expanding it to whatever level they feel is needed.

    • Ed, NI5R, has volunteered to write the basic emergency plan for the club.

    • WA0KAQ noted that there should be a few more back-up batteries available after Field Day for distribution to members who are actively involved in being on the air and checking into the nets.
