PWARC Bake Sale Is Umm Umm Good!
Pueblo West Walmart -- May 23, 2010
(Photo by Bill, WA0KAQ)

Thanks for all the time and effort from NE0DA, ND0P, KB0RPD, NG0T, WA0KAQ, KA0USA, KB0JIT, KA0RER, W0NHF, KC0QJK, KG6WSI, NI5R and KD0LDH who participated in making our club's spring Bake Sale a great success! Jane (KA0USA, left in photo), as the Ways and Means Committee, set a goal to bring $300 into the club's treasury. Sales were $276.00, and we received $17.20 in donations for a total of $293.20. In another half an hour, we would probably have sold enough to top the goal, but by then we had lost our shade from the building and the items on the last table were getting quite sparse.

When we packed up shortly after 1 p.m., Paul (KB0JIT) had a few muffins left over that he gave to Jane. When Jane saw the balance from the sale, she took some muffins and cookies to a neighbor's home and sold them for an additional $7.00. (Jane's OM Bill, WA0KAQ, thought she was going to take away a cherry pie Ty (KG6WSI) brought and that Bill bought, but Jane finally relented and let Bill keep it. HI HI ) Now how's that for determination? The treasury will be receiving $300.20, and rest of the cookies that were left over (almost 7 dozen) are in the freezer and will be taken to Field Day as part of our free refreshments.

Thanks to Sharron Fortune (in black in photo above; XYL of Bill, W0NHF) and 6-year-old Benji Lloyd (son of Andy, ND0P) for their sales help. Benji was especially helpful in selling Whoopie Pies!

Thanks also to Ed Force (NI5R) for making such a nice frame for our club banner. It looked and worked great and will be a nice asset for our Field Day event as well.

 Bake Sale and Portable Radio Demonstration
Pueblo West Walmart -- October 24, 2009
(Photos by Bill, WA0KAQ)

Despite a chilly wind, the bake sale started about 8:15 a.m. instead of the scheduled 9:00. As soon goodies started to be placed on the tables, buyers began to appear. Sharon Fortune (XYL of W0NHF) Jane (KE6DCE) and Marge Schoenhofen (a non-member friend of Jane's) took over the sales duties. Shown behind the table (l-r) are Sharon Fortune, Jane (KE6DCE), Bill (KC0QJK), Marge Schoenhofen, and Andy (AB8HQ). Others members participating included Paul (KB0JIT), Bill (WA0KAQ), Bill (W0NHF), Bill (KB0RPD), and Jim (NE0DA). Despite a recent bout with double pneumonia and shingles, Ralph (KA0RER) also stopped by for a few minutes to contribute to the cause.

Hmmmm... there was almost too much from which to choose. These customers had a difficult time deciding between the cakes, brownies, cookies, pies and more. The goodies didn't last long.

This customer took an interest in the sign about our club made by Andy, AB8HQ (light brown jacket) and chatted with Paul, KB0JIT about ham radio. Paul (r) talked with those interested in the club.

Sometimes customers are just plain fun to serve. These two hunting buddies provided some good-hearted entertainment for the club members including Jane, KE6DCE (now KA0USA).

Bill, W0NHF, (left) brought his new Yaesu FT-897D transceiver with the internal power supply and matching automatic antenna tuner and hooked it up as a portable unit. A Webster Bandspanner mobile antenna on a sturdy magmount antenna base was mounted on roof of a pickup truck to complete the station setup.

Bill is shown here explaining the operation of the transceiver to his son Bill, KB0RPD, (center) and to Jim, NE0DA.

Bill, KB0RPD, tunes for stations operating SSB in the CQ WW DX Contest, while Paul, KB0JIT, (left) talks with a prospective ham.
