Club Donates CQ Magazine Subscription To Library
April 17, 2010

In recognition of National Library Month, Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club presented a 1-year subscription to CQ Magazine to Pueblo West Library during the club's April 17 meeting. Club president Jim McConnell (NE0DA) made the presentation of the April issue of the magazine to librarian Cindy Shimizu, along with a Certificate of Recognition.

President McConnell expressed his appreciation for the support the club has received from Pueblo West Library, as well as the excellent facilities it provides for the club's use.

Ms. Shimizo thanked the club for the donation and stated the monthly issues will be placed in clear plastic covers and will be labeled as being provided by Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club.

Three Club Members Upgrade Licenses
February 13, 2010

Three Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club members upgraded their licenses at a VE Session sponsored by Pueblo Ham Club on February 13, 2010.

Ed Force, KD5MHI, (left) upgraded from General to Amateur Extra. He subsequently changed his call to NI5R.

Jane Ewing, KE6DCE, (center) upgraded from Technician to General and soon received her Vanity Call, KA0USA.

Bill Fortune III, KB0RPD, (right) upgraded from Technician to General.

Congratulations to all three members!

Is It A New Record?

WA0KAQ thinks he may have set a new record, but he doesn't think it is a good one. He started hunting U.S. counties back in 1964 when he was first licensed in Nebraska. After 43 years of being on and off the air, due mostly to antenna restrictions, he finally finished the task on August 2, 2007 when he worked the last of the 3.077 counties. He now holds a mixed mode USA-CA Worked All Counties Certificate #1157, issued September 3, 2007.

Bill is one of two members of the Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club to have achieved the award. The other is Dan, NG0T, who has awards for working all the counties mixed mode, and another for working them all on CW! Their paths didn't cross when they were working for the awards, but they made many friends in common through the years.

Dan Broadbanks, NG0T, loves to operate CW, but he didn't tell us that he had worked all the U.S. counties using Morse Code. He qualified for the USA-CW Award in 1984, receiving USA-CW #6. He also holds USA-CA #341 for working all the counties on mixed modes. Well done, Dan!

This photo of Dan was taken at the Double Tree Hotel in Aurora, CO, during the County Hunters' National Convention in Denver in 2007.
