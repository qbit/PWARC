What Equipment Is Needed To Operate VHF Packet Radio?

While some stations use sound card interfaces with their computer, this option does not seem to be a good one in our district. Configuring the software has been a large problem. Therefore, most stations in our district have turned to TNCs (Terminal Node Connectors). Used TNCs can be found at swap meets and on e-bay. The most popular models seem to made by Kantronics (KPC-2, KPC-3) and AEA (PK-232MBX, PK-900).

VHF Packet Equipment:

    Two-meter transceiver (even a handheld running 5 watts will work if you can "see" a node). Older rigs without tone capability can be usually be picked up quite reasonably at swapfests.
    Radio to TNC cable - Many available from MFJ for about $16.00; others can be made from microphone cables or from "scratch."
    USED TNC - $50-$175, depending on manufacturer and model. Fellow hams may also have an extra TNC for sale.
    RS-232 Null Modem Cable - to connect TNC to computer com port - $10 (?)
    Computer - Older, slower computers work great for VHF packet. Some software is happy working with Windows 3.1 machines (286, 386, 486); some software requires Windows 95, 98+. Pentium I machines work great for all packet software. Laptops make for great portability, but desktops are fine for home use. Many times you may find one of these "outdated" machines free for the asking.
    Software - Some are free on Internet, and many programs are available at up to $50 -- Ask what others are using. WinPack is free on the Internet and is becoming an ARES standard.
    Two-meter antenna - Depending on your location, a "spike," j-pole or mag-mount antenna indoors may work. In other locations, an outside antenna may be required.
    12-volt Power Supply with power cables to run your radio and TNC
    Printer - When working with agencies, a printer will be needed to provide hard copy information of message traffic.

Before you buy, visit someone who is active on packet radio. Check out the equipment and software that person is using, and ask questions.
