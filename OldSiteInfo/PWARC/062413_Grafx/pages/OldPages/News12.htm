News from the members: (What have you done in the last month that pertains to amateur radio?)

    Working with the local schools:

      Bill, KB0RPD, reported that he, Paul, KB0JIT, and Ralph, KA0RER, met on December 9 with Todd Seip, 6th Grade Science teacher at Skyview Middle School. The meeting centered on forming a partnership between the Skyview school's science department and PWARC with the intention of promoting science and math through Amateur Radio. Mr. Seip was enthusiastic about the possibility of bringing Amateur Radio into the middle school.

      Several options were discussed but the group decided that as a first exposure, PWARC would bring a demonstration of Amateur Radio to the middle school. The demonstration was tentatively set for Thursday, January 27, at 10 AM. PWARC would set up 3 stations running SSB, CW, and PSK31.

      The demonstration would take place in a 6th grade science room where there is access through a window to the outside for antennas. All 6th grade science classes, and the students from the gifted/talented program would attend, and this could bring the number of students to more than 200. The demonstration would last for about 1 hour.

       Following this demonstration the team will discuss with Mr. Seip the issue of coordinating a project for the gifted/talented students at Skyview.

      Resource requirements: radios that are battery operated, antennas and coax, laptops for PSK31 and internet access, operators

       Club members were enthusiastic about the possibility of exposing more youth to the hobby, though some of the details may need to be changed. The subject of possibly making the event a 2-hour demonstration was discussed, due to the large number of students involved. KB0RPD will be working on more details with Mr. Seip and will be asking for input and ideas from club members. A sign-up sheet will be sent to members who can participate or provide equipment. Details can be finalized at the January 15 meeting.

    KB0JIT, who is also a member of the Pueblo Ham Club, stated that at their last meeting they asked him to find out our club's feeling about having a joint Field Day. The consensus the club came to was that Field Day is a single club exercise designed to test that club's ability to respond to portable Field Operations, learn new modes, and test equipment. It is also a time for members to get together to bond as a club and have fun together. Therefore, the club is not interested in holding a joint Field Day exercise. However, should Pueblo Ham Club care to have a joint Special Event Station or a joint Fox Hunt in the future, our members would probably be agreeable to that.
