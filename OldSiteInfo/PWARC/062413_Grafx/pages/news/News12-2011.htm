ARRL Membership Report:

    The club has 33 members, 32 of whom are licensed. Of those 8 hold Technician licenses, 7 have General Licenses, 2 hold Advanced licenses, and 15 have Amateur Extra licenses. Of the 32 voting members, 22 belong to ARRL. (66.7%).

New ARRL Memberships or Renewals: None

News from the members: (What have you done in the last month pertaining to amateur radio?)

  • Jeff, KC0CTN, recently purchased a Wouxun UHF/VHF handheld as an early Christmas present and has been checking into the club's weekly nets.

  • Neal, KD0MBL, has been spending a lot of time on 2 meters and experimenting with antennas. He has also finished taking FEMA's free on-line courses ICS-100 (Introduction to Incident Command System) and ICS-200 (ICS for Resources and Initial Action Incidents).

  • Bill, W0NHF, reported he has programmed his Yaesu FT-890D for the club's emergency channels. He also has interfaced his radio to his computer using Ham Radio Deluxe software and is using a large monitor to make viewing easier.

  • Bill, KB0RPD, repaired his 10 meter antenna and worked a few stations during the ARRL 10 meter Contest.

  • Jim, N0OBV, said he had mostly been working on test equipment.

  • Ed, NI5R, made about 22 contacts in the ARRL 10 meter Contest.

  • Stan, KC0OYO, reported making contacts on 10, 17 and 40 meters.

  • Amanda, K1DDN, reports band conditions on 40 meters have been poor, but she has been involved in her regular 40 meter net. She said husband Jeff, K0JSC, has also be playing with EchoLink.

  • Dan, NG0T, scored a personal best in the CQ World Wide CW Contest with a claimed score of 303,000 points. He may have also worked DXCC during the weekend.

  • Paul, KB0JIT, reported 28 contacts in the ARRL 10 Meter Contest. He also finished building a solar controller that Neal, KD0MBL, is testing.

  • Jane, KA0USA, worked 25 stations in the ARRL 10 Meter Contest.

  • Jane reported husband Bill, WA0KAQ, has not been active in December. However, he entered the November Sweepstakes (Phone) contest. He worked 270 stations in 77 of the 80 districts for a total of 41,580 points. He also received QSL cards from China, Hong Kong, South Korea and Ascension Island in the past month.
