The club welcomes new member Ty Luck, KG6WSI, to the club. Ty was working with Search and Rescue and was not able to attend. He joined the club at the April 3 Ragchew.

Members signed and presented a sympathy card to Ed (NI5R) on the recent loss of his mother in Washington State. A get well card was also signed and will be sent to Janice Vaerewyck (KC0UKU), wife of Gene (K9HBR), who fell recently and broke several bones in her arm.

Treasurer Dan Broadbooks (NG0T) stated the club's treasury stood at $409.07 on April 1. Two membership checks for $15 each and a $20 contribution from Stan Deyo for Field Day were presented to Dan today. A check for $50.35 was paid to WA0KAQ as reimbursements for the club's vanity call ($13.40) and the subscription the club purchased for Pueblo West Library ($36.95).

Jane (KA0USA) of the Ways and Means Committee reminded the members that the club will hold a bake sale fundraiser on Sunday, March 23, at the Pueblo West Walmart. Sign-ups for goodies were taken, but more will be needed from members who did not attend today's meeting. Jim, NE0DA, will also provide a shade.

Training Coordinators Paul (KB0JIT) and Ed (NI5R) reported that the class has produced two new hams to date. In March, Randy (KD0KVA) obtained his Technician License, and Kirk (KD0LDH) passed is Technician exam on April 10. Congratulations to both new hams! With 8 weeks to go until the next examination, the coordinators hope to have more prospects ready for their Technician licenses and upgrades.

A VE session will be held from 10 a.m.-12:00 noon on Saturday, June 12, at the Rawlings Library, 100 E. Abriendo Ave., Pueblo. The test session will be conducted by the Pueblo Ham Club, with two Volunteer Examiners from Pueblo West also participating. At the April 10 session, KB0JIT and WA0KAQ represented our club as VEs. Our club's newest VE is Jane, (KA0USA). She received her VE credentials on April 14. Anyone who is interested in becoming a Volunteer Examiner is asked to contact WA0KAQ or one of the other local VEs.

WA0KAQ reported that Jane (KA0USA) has joined District 16 ARES. The club now has six members who are enrolled in ARES.

Items Still Tabled:

    1) Club's need for a Board of Directors? - Dan, NG0T, who did our incorporation paperwork, explained we are not a 501(3)c corporation, but he will research this issue further. The item was tabled at February meeting.

The club is awaiting word from ARRL as to whether its application for ARRL Club Affiliation has been accepted. WA0KAQ sent documents requesting the affiliation to the ARRL Executive Committee on April 6. To become affiliated and retain affiliation, 51% of the voting members must be ARRL members. KA0USA is our club's newest ARRL member, bringing our percentage to 64% as of April 6. The affiliation must be approved by the Colorado Section Communications Manager, the Rocky Mountain Division Director, and the ARRL Executive Committee in order for the club to be given affiliation. After we receive the affiliation, if anyone wants to join or renew a membership, please do it through the club, as we can retain a portion of most fees for the club's treasury.

In recognition of National Library Month, Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club presented a 1-year subscription to CQ Magazine to Pueblo West Library during the club's April 17 meeting. Club president Jim McConnell (NE0DA) made the presentation of the April issue of the magazine to librarian Cindy Shimizu, along with a Certificate of Recognition. (See Photos page on website)

President McConnell expressed the club's appreciation for the support it has received from Pueblo West Library, as well as the excellent facilities it provides. Ms. Shimizo thanked the club for the donation and stated the monthly issues will be placed in clear plastic covers and will be labeled as being provided by Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club.

The Pueblo West Committee On Architecture approved the club's application for Field Day signs at its April 8 meeting. We now have the paperwork from them.

The club's FD Handbook has been sent to members by e-mail. Some appendixes have also been sent, while others await information from the membership. If you have not done so, please print the Annex N you received (Field Day Questionnaire), fill it out, and send it by mail or e-mail to WA0KAQ. Only two questionnaires have been received (ND0P & WG6WSI).

With only 69 days until Field Day, invitations are starting to be distributed. KB0RPD was given the invitation for the American Red Cross at today's meeting. Other invitations will soon be ready for distribution.

NE0DA and WA0KAQ met with Larry Howe-Kerr, Manager of the Pueblo West Metropolitan District on April 9. They presented Howe-Kerr and his staff a formal invitation to Field Day. They requested a proclamation proclaiming the last week of June as Amateur Radio Week in Pueblo West. Howe-Kerr will present the request to the Pueblo West Metropolitan Board for their approval. If the Board approves, the proclamation will be presented in early June so the announcement can be made in the newspaper the week of Field Day.

Bill (KB0RPD) will be checking on the used USB batteries we requested from the NWS. The batteries will be needed in May so the club can begin experimenting with them for the Field Day exercise.

Field Day always seems to be a weekend of high temperatures. One of the things we will use to try to entice visitors to Field Day is to offer FREE refreshments, which will consist of cookies and iced tea or ice water. Andy (KD0P) has volunteered the use of a 5-gallon dispenser, and Jim (NE0DA) volunteered an ice chest and ice. More ice cubes will be needed, so if you can help out with this, please contact Jane (KA0USA).

Andy (ND0P) reported that work on the two Field Day signs is progressing. They will each say "Visitors Welcome!" above "Free Refreshments!" The top section of each that says "Visitors Welcome!" can be taken off so they can be used separately at other events.

WA0KAQ announced that the club's Field Day site has been set on the ARRL's new Field Day Locator web page. In fact, it is listed twice. The site can be found at http://www.arrl.org/field-day-locator.

The program consisted of the video "A Kansas Nightmare" about the April 1991 tornado that hit Wichita, Kansas, after which Bill (KB0RPD) presented an interesting one-hour Storm Spotter Training program.
