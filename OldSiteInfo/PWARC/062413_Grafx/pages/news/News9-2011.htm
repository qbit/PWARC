ARRL Membership Report:

  • The club has 34 members, 33 of whom are licensed. Of those 11 hold Technician licenses, 6 have General licenses, 2 hold Advanced licenses, and 14 have Amateur Extra licenses. Of the 33 voting members, 22 belong to ARRL. (66.7%).

New ARRL Memberships or Renewals: None

News from the members: (What have you done in the last month pertaining to amateur radio?)

  • Amanda, K1DDN, reported she had done little on the radio in the last month.

  • Jim, NE0DA, has been working PSK31 and CW. He contacted 15 stations during the CO QSO Party. He also helped put up antennas for NI5R.

  • Jim, N0BVD, has a new MFJ sound card interface. He is trying to establish computer control of his Yaesu antenna rotor. He also served as a VE at the exam session today.

  • Bob, WD0FBW, has not been on the air but is looking for an HF rig.

  • Lori, AH6OS, reported no radio contacts but helped with today's VE Session.

  • Paul, KB0JIT, replaced a broken connector on the coax for his vertical and worked Hungary on 15 meters for his next contact.

  • Bill, W0NHF, says the DX bug bit him. He worked more than 40 stations in Europe, Asia and the Azores on 15 and 17 meters.

  • Neal, KD0MBL, Made a few contacts in the CO QSO Party. He also helped put up antennas for NI5R and helped at today's VE Session.

  • Dan, NG0T, received several QSL cards from the Bureau including one from Chatham Island and one from Madagascar. He entered the CQ World Wide QSO Party and placed fifth in the "0" call district on CW.

  • David, KY6YYY, continues to work DX stations. He also worked special event stations for Route 66, K4MIA, and the Salem Sue event in North Dakota. He also helped at today's VE Session.

  • Jane, KA0USA, entered the Colorado QSO Party with WA0KAQ as a multi-operator, single transmitter entry. She reported they made 119 contacts. She also worked Al from Coaldale when he was operating the W9IMS special event station in Indianapolis, IN. She also worked special event station K4MIA in Florida. She received QSL cards from CA, OR, KY and the Slovak Republic during the last month.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, has been cleaning up the main ham shack and adding some features back that had been dropped in the past. He now has an old 2-meter radio running Packet and APRS through a TNC again and have hooked up a couple of old tower computers for logging and digital modes. He also worked Al from Coaldale when Al was in Indianapolis. He worked Portugal and the Dominican Republic on PSK31.

  • Andy, ND0P, reported he has a new Yaesu FT-60 dual-band handheld.

Old Business:

  1. Non Profit Corporation Report

    • Dan, NG0T, reported the IRS has approved the club as a 503(c)(3) non-profit organization. It will take approximately 100 days for the listing to show up on the IRS website; however we have the official letter from the IRS.

    • Dan will check to see if club dues are deductible.

  2. Scholarship

    • WA0KAQ reported that as part of our incorporation as a non-profit charity, it is our obligation to put some of the money we make back into projects that will help the community. Earlier it was decided that one way to do this would be to offer a scholarship to a graduating senior from Pueblo West High School who intends to pursue an education in the field of science or electronics. Graduation will be May 24, 2012.

    • Bill contacted Joann Turner at Pueblo West High School about the scholarship. What she needs are the requirements, and she will then contact the students who qualify. It is then up to the students to apply.

    • As set up, the scholarship would not be available to club members or their relatives. NE0DA questioned this and does not think this should be the case. The exception was included in the requirements to meet the IRS's ruling that no one from a club should profit from club funds. NG0T will check for a clarification of the IRS requirements and report at the next meeting.

    • Jim (NE0DA) made a motion that the amount of the scholarship should be $250 for 2012. Paul (KB0JIT) seconded the motion. The motion was approved with a unanimous vote.

  3. Run The Rim 5K and 10K Run, Royal Gorge Bridge (WA0KAQ)

    • Royal Gorge Ham Club President Derrick Houska, N0TQ, has invited members of our club to participate in the annual Run The Rim event at Royal Gorge Bridge on Saturday, Oct. 1. Altitude for this event varies from approximately 6,500' to 6,850'.

    • The race begins on the north side of the bridge at 8:30 a.m. and should be over about 11 a.m. The event will use the 145.490 Royal Gorge Ham Club repeater. The backup simplex frequency is 147.550.

    • KD0MBL, ND0P, AH6OS and NE0DA volunteered to help with the event.

New Business:

  1. Upcoming nominations

    • The club's Constitution states "In October, the Board of Directors and the current president of the Executive Committee shall appoint a Nominations Committee. By the November general meeting the committee will have chosen a slate of nominees to the Executive Committee, at least one for each office, and for the Board of Directors. At the November meeting nominations will be opened to the floor. After the close of the nominations, the Nominating Committee will add its selections to the slate." - We didn't do this last year, as we were changing the Constitution about that time. So this year we need to adhere to the Constitution. Therefore, the Board of Directors and the club President will select a Nominations Committee by the October meeting.

  2. Upcoming Audit

    • The Constitution provides for the auditing of the club's financial records in December each year. It further states that the Board will "oversee the Club financial structure and activity, including income, expenses, insurance coverage, audits, bank relations, fund-raising, and other financial procedure." Having the Board audit the books seems to be a natural progression of that statement.

    • David (KY6YYY) made a motion that the Board conduct the annual audit in December. Lori (AH6OS) seconded the motion. The motion was approved.

  3. Honorary Memberships

These may include such persons as emergency responders, law enforcement personnel, and club donors.
