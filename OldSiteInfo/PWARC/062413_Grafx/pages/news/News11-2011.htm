ARRL Membership Report:

The club has 33 members, 32 of whom are licensed. Of those 10 hold Technician licenses, 6 have General Licenses, 2 hold Advanced licenses, and 14 have Amateur Extra licenses. Of the 32 voting members, 22 belong to ARRL. (68.75%).

News from the members: (What have you done in the last month pertaining to amateur radio?)

  • Amanda, K1DDN, reported she did her usual HF nets and also worked some DX including Australia, New Zealand and Puerto Rico.

  • Jim, N0OBV, was gone to the East Coast the first part of the month and had not been active. He is still having power line noise problems on the HF frequencies.

  • Mason, WA6HSI, brought a miniature homebrew 10-meter CW transmitter that he had built in a Sucrets box.

  • Ted, K0LDS, has been working DX and also participated in the ARRL November Sweepstakes (CW) contest. He is working to establish a contesting group within our club.

  • Ed, NI5R, has been chasing DX.

  • Paul, KB0JIT, entered the Telephone Pioneers Contest. He is also building two projects - a 3-element 2-meter quad antenna and a solar charge controller.

  • Jim, NE0DA, is chasing DX again after replacing a broken U-bolt on his yagi antenna.

  • Bill, KB0RPD, reported his coax shorted out after a recent storm. He will need to bring it inside to re-solder it.

  • Dan, NG0T, entered the November Sweepstakes (CW) contest so that the club would have the required third entry.

  • David, KY6YYY, has just returned from two weeks in North Dakota. He has been working DX and also recently received a certificate from W9IMS for working special event stations for each of the three annual races in Indianapolis.

  • Jane, KA0USA, worked a Special Event Station commemorating Nevada Statehood and also had a nice QSO with a mobile station in North Dakota on SSB. She received QSL cards from Texas, and from W9IMS (International Motor Speedway).

    On PSK31, she worked Texas, Arkansas, Washington, Illinois, Arizona, Mexico, Oregon, Vermont, Idaho, Canada, Georgia, Maryland, New York, Florida, Argentina, and Japan. She also worked Dwight (WB0HBJ) on PSK31 when he was home.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, trimmed his 10-meter dipole just a bit before the snow came in October, and it is working very nicely now throughout the band. He reported he had had probably the best month ever on HF.

    He worked 40 countries on 10 meters one afternoon during the CQ WW DX Contest on Oct. 30. The contacts were mostly in Europe, South America, and the Caribbean. No new countries were worked.

    On RTTY, running about 35 watts, he worked E. Kiribati, Wales, New Zealand, Latvia, Cuba, Luxembourg, Spain, England, the Netherlands, Belgium, Morocco, Germany, and St. Eustatius Island, but again worked no new countries.

    Though PSKS-125 is not used much because it takes more bandwidth, he saw a PSK-125 signal and ended up working France.

    On SSB, Bill worked England, Australia, Guam, Marquesas Island, S. Cook Island, and re-worked Fiji. He also worked Mariana Islands, Sable Island, Ivory Coast, and the island of Niue for the first time.

    PSK-31 brought contacts with Serbia, South Africa, Alaska, and England, as well as the countries of Romania, South Korea and China for the first time.

    Though not a CW buff, he worked S. Korea and China, and reworked Fiji. He also managed contacts with Hong Kong, Western Malaysia, Ascension Island, Tuvalu and Egypt for the first time. He has received a LOTW confirmation from the Western Malaysia contact.

    Bill worked 12 new countries since the last meeting and has now passed the half way point in working all the countries. There were 341 entities as of July 2011, and he has worked 177 of them. His goal is to reach 200 confirmed, and they are getting more difficult to find.

    Bill entered the November Sweepstakes contest to help out with the club's first contest effort. He worked 65 of the 80 sections and made 200 contacts running 40 watts the majority of the time for an individual score of 26,000 points.

Old Business:

  • New divisions within our General Fund

    • A discussion was held about making divisions within the club's General Fund. It was decided to only make divisions where they are necessary. The club decided to make two divisions to the club's General Fund. These are for Equipment, and Scholarships. One hundred dollars received as a donation for the sale of a radio will be added to the Equipment Fund. The club voted earlier to set aside $250 for a scholarship, and this money will be placed in the Scholarship Fund.

  • Clarification of IRS stand on eligibility for scholarships

    • While no definite answer has yet been found regarding whether a club member or a relative of a club member can be eligible for one of our Pueblo West High School graduate scholarships, every attempt to conform to IRS rules will be made.

    • The club's Board of Directors will be responsible for choosing the scholarship recipient (if any). David (KY6YYY) made a motion to this affect, and Ed (NI5R) seconded the motion. A unanimous vote followed. Any applications received will be turned over to the board without identifying information.

    • Bill (WA0KAQ) stated that since the club is interested in attracting youth as members, it would be nice for them to be able to apply for the scholarships to compete with other candidates. Since no identification would be on the applications, it would not be known if their applications were being reviewed in the final decision.

  • Changes to Scholarship

    • At the October meeting, there was some concern that the scholarship requirements didn't require more of a field of study in electronics for applicants. Since then, the eligibility has been narrowed to those seeking a degree in one of the following fields:

      • Electronics Technology

      • Electronics Engineering
      • Electrical Engineering
      • Broadcast Engineering
      • Education, with emphasis on teaching science

    • The due date for applications is now March 3.

    • A clause was inserted so that if no suitable applications are received, the club can retain the funds for future use.

New Business:

  • Battery dispersal

    • Four more 18-Ah batteries were available for distribution. One each went to KD0MBL, NE0DA, K0LDS, and WA6HSI.

  • Advanced Emergency Plan

    • In addition to the Basic Emergency Plan that was set up for club members, there is also an advanced plan for those who want to be involved. This plan provides for the possibility of providing back-up communications for public service agencies or other groups in case they are needed. The plan is quite lengthy, and it involves occasional training exercises. It also involves setting up more digital communications which may include training in FLdigi and its plug-in programs, APRS, PSK-31, Packet, RMS Express, SSTV, and traffic handling and more.

    • Ed (NI5R) is in charge of the plan and training exercises. The first training session will be held in January. Those whoare interested in participating in the emergency preparedness team that can benefit the community were asked to contact Ed to be signed up. Ed will set up the first training session at the new LDS Church.
