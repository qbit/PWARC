News from the members: (What have you done in the last month that pertains to amateur radio?)

  • Stan, KC0OYO, reported he received good news from the Pueblo West Metropolitan Board's Committee On Architecture. The Committee approved his tower and beam, plus two vertical antennas on his garage, and a loop antenna.

  • David, KY6YYY, has been designing his own QSL cards.

  • Paul, KB0JIT, has been refurbishing WA0KAQ's Butternut HF6V vertical and building a counterpoise for it. He brought photos of the antenna with its homebrew counterpoise and 4" lightweight PVC pipe protecting the 80 and 40 meter coils. It is looking good! Now he needs more warm days to begin tuning it on the six bands.

  • Bob, WD0FBW, has installed a 2-meter rig in his vehicle and has applied for his retirement. Now he will have more time to be on the air!

  • Neal, KD0MBL, has been working PSK31 with a SignalLink sound card interface. He also recently received accreditation as a Volunteer Examiner and put his skills to use at the VE Session held before today's meeting.

  • Bill, W0NHF, has been enjoying Ragchews on 15 meters.

  • Ralph, KA0RER, recently worked Moose Island, ME, which is good for four Lighthouse Award contacts.

  • Bill, KB0RPD, found time to be on 20 meter SSB last week, working the west coast.

  • Stanley, WB0JRG, has been studying fractal antennas.

  • Ed, NI5R, has received confirmation of his 50th state (Delaware) and will soon be applying for the ARRL Worked All States (WAS) award.

  • Dan, NG0T, received several cards from the ARRL QSL Bureau, including one from rare Lichtenstein.

  • Jane, KA0USA, received QSL cards from Texas, Alabama, Martinique and Barbados. She also renewed her ARRL membership.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, reported he and Paul, KB0JIT, went to Colorado Springs the last week in December where he purchased a used Cushcraft MA5B mini-beam. He has also mounted an MFJ attenuator on KA0USA's tape measure Yagi. He reported he had received a QSL from a CW contact with Bonaire and now has worked 153 countries and has cards from 145 of them.

Membership Report: (Updated since meeting report)

Alan McFadden, from Alamosa, joined the club before this meeting after passing his Technician exam earlier in the afternoon. Alan will have his Technician license shortly. The club currently has 30 members, with 26 voting (licensed) members. Of the 26 voting members, 17 (65.4%) are ARRL members. The club now has members holding 8 Technician licenses, 6 General licenses, 2 Advanced licenses, and 10 Amateur Extra licenses.

New ARRL Memberships or Renewals: Bill, W0NHF (Renewal)

Ways & Means Report:

To help raise money for future club activities, Jane, KA0USA, presented a raffle for a nice homebrew 6-element 70 cm beam made and donated by Paul, KB0JIT. KA0USA was the lucky winner. The raffle brought in $20.00 for the treasury. Thanks go out to Paul for his donation.

The club will continue to hold raffles or offer items for direct sale at the meetings until the summer months arrive and it can hold other fundraising events. All proceeds go to the club. Any donation of items for raffle will be appreciated.

The raffle item for the February meeting will be the book "Joe Carr's Loop Antenna Handbook, Your Guide to Large and Small Loop Antennas for Transmitting and Receiving" by Joseph J. Carr, K4IPV. This raffle item is from Bill, WA0KAQ.

Report from Instructors:

Ed, NI5R, noted he has been tasked by his church to hold amateur radio classes on three Saturdays in January. The classes would be held at the Latter Day Saints Church, 4720 Surfwood Lane, Pueblo. The classes will run from 9:00 a.m. to 1 p.m. on January 22 & 29 and February 5. No affiliation with the church is required, so anyone wishing to attend may do so.

Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club will sponsor a VE Session on February 12 from 1:00 to 3:00 p.m. in King Room B at the Pueblo West Library. Walk-ins will be accepted until 2:00 p.m. More information is available on the club's website.

NI5R reported he had an inquiry from a Scout leader about helping some Scouts earn their Radio Merit Badge. He responded to the request, however he has received no reply from the Scoutmaster.

VE Session Report:

Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club sponsored a VE Session from Noon-2:00 p.m. preceding the January meeting. Four candidates tested, and three passed their examinations. Ray Miles passed the Technician test, as did Alan McFadden who came from Alamosa to take his exam. Jack Magill of Canon City passed the Technician, General and Amateur Extra exams. Eight Volunteer Examiners from Pueblo West and Pueblo attended the VE Session.

Report on Ragchew:

Due to the New Year's holiday, no Ragchew was scheduled in January. The next Ragchew will be February 5.

Old Business:

  • Non-profit corporation progress:

    • Dan, NG0T, reported he will be send the paperwork to the IRS after the new club officers sign the signature card at the bank at 10:00 a.m. Tuesday, January 18. The application fee of $400 was approved by the members several months ago, and the check was written at the December 2010 meeting.

  • Working with the local schools:

    • Bill, KB0RPD, reported that more contacts have been made with Todd Seip, 6th Grade Science teacher at Skyview Middle School and the grounds were walked to determine a good location for antennas.

    • Since the ARRL School Club Roundup is scheduled for February 14-18, plans are now being made to have the ham radio presentation on Friday, February 18. There is no school on Friday; however Mr. Seip's Talented and Gifted students routinely are scheduled for activities on Fridays.

    • The presentation will be held in the school's library from 10:00 a.m. to 2:00 p.m. The activity is mainly for the Talented and Gifted students, however all Skyview Middle School students will be invited. It is estimated that there will be about 50 students attend the event throughout the 4-hour period.

    • While the school does not have a radio club, non-school clubs and individuals are encouraged to participate in the ARRL School Club Roundup.

    • Bill stated Mr. Seip may be interested in obtaining a ham license, and it is hoped this presentation will lead to a club being formed in the future. Ed, NI5R, suggested the club purchase a Gordon West License Manual and loan it to Mr. Seip. If the influential teacher would get his ham license, it is felt several students may follow his example. Ed made a motion to purchase the license manual, and David, KY6YYY, seconded the motion. The motion was approved on a unanimous voice vote. Ed will purchase the book and be reimbursed by the club.

    • The Roundup has specific rules pertaining to multi-transmitter operation. PWARC would set up stations for Phone, CW and PSK-31, however only one transmitter can be used at a time. The ideal situation would be to have one student talking and one student logging while using the phone transceiver. The operators will oversee the transmissions and will use the club's NA0PW call sign.

    • Tentative plans are to make another presentation in October, as the ARRL School Club Roundup will again be held the week of Oct. 17-21. Whether the presentation will be made at Skyview Middle School or another school is still to be determined.

    • KA0RPD reported that the item most in need for this event is operators. If you can help, contact him.

    • Transceivers and antennas have been secured. David, KY6YYY, volunteered to bring 75' of coax. More may be needed.

    • Display materials will also be needed. Two collages of photos taken at Field Day by KY6YYY, a tri-fold information poster made by KA0USA for Field Day, and the club banner are available for use. David will make copies of a kids' brochure that is now out of stock at ARRL.

  • Club Promotion

    • At the December meeting, Bill, WA0KAQ, was asked to inquire at the Chamber of Commerce as to whether there is a cost to have an entry in the annual Fourth of July Parade. Bill was told there is no entry fee. There are some rules for entries however, and a liability release form to sign. Entries must be in by approximately July 1.

    • It was the consensus of the club members that an entry in the parade would be beneficial. Ed, NI5R, volunteered the use of a 16' flatbed trailer for the parade float.

    • Plans for the parade entry will be discussed at future meetings.

  • Fourth of July Booth

    • Jane, KA0USA, reported that she checked on the price of a booth during the July 4 celebration. Having a booth is too expensive for our purposes. Craft booths cost $50 for non-Chamber members for only 5 hours of use, including the parade which takes most of the potential customers out of the booth area. Food booths are $150 for non-Chamber members.

David, KY6YYY, had been thinking along the same line and has started to design a logo for the club to consider. The logo would be something that could be used on T-Shirts, hats, or made into patches. More information is forthcoming.

Community Service Suggestions:

  • Liberty Point 5K Run and 5K Walk - July 4, 2011

    • WA0KAQ was directed at the December meeting to contact United Way of Pueblo and ask if they would like back-up communications for their event. Bill talked to race coordinator Bianca Hicks, who was very excited about having our club participate. Last year the number of participants was 240 runners and 61 who walked the course. This was double the number who participated the year before, and Hicks anticipates this year's event may be even larger. As the race planning progresses, she and Bill will be in contact to determine the club's role in the event.

  • Scholarship

    • As a Non-Profit Organization, our club needs to use some of its funds to help the community. WA0KAQ suggested the club might consider making a small scholarship available in the future to a Pueblo West High School graduate who intends to major in science in college, or better yet to teach science after college graduation.

  • Donation to the Pueblo West Library

    • The club made a donation of a CQ Magazine subscription to the library last year in April for National Library Week. WA0KAQ asked if the members had taken advantage of having the magazine available, and if this should be done again this year, or whether something else might be more suitable. A few members said they read the magazine, some monthly. Billy, K5LKT, suggested buying an ARRL Handbook in place of the subscription. No decision was made, and this will item brought up at a future meeting.

  • A suggestion was made that the club might help with community clean-up drives or a highway clean-up project.

  • Anyone having other suggestions on how to serve the community are invited to share them at meetings of direct them to WA0KAQ for inclusion on the monthly agendas.
