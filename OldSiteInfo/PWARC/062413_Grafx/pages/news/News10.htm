News From Members:

Andy, ND0P, reported he and Neal have been doing some direction finding using Neal's tape measure antenna.

Jim, NE0DA, worked some CA QSO Party stations and built a tape measure yagi.

Bill, WA0KAQ, had a good radio month. He said he decided he wasn't setting a good example for the club since he hadn't been on the air much in the last three months, so he spent more time on the radio the last few weeks.

  • PSK31

    • He worked Cuba on 20 meters, and Italy on 15 meters. The most interesting PSK31 contact was with a station from Russia who was on a pipe laying barge off the coast of eastern Russia. They were laying a natural gas line under the ocean.
  • SSB
    • He worked Moldova again on 20 meters. He worked the same ham 3 years ago but never got a card.
    • He worked Bonaire from new DX entities formed from the Netherlands Antilles.
    • He worked East Malaysia, Brunei, Togo and Antarctica on 20 meters.
    • On Sept 30 he heard a station in the Slovak Republic calling CQ on 15 meters. He didn't need the country but went back to him. Immediately after he worked him, Dwight, WB0HBJ, worked him. Both received the same signal report. Bill was using a 40 meter dipole that is laying on the roof at one point, and Dwight was using a 30' vertical.
  • CW
    • His big catches for the month were Mongolia and Zimbabwe on 20 meters.
    • He worked the new entities of St Maarten, and Saba and St Eustatius after the breakup of the Netherlands Antilles.
  • Miscellaneous
    • Three QSL cards came since the last meeting. One was from Greece, and one was from Brunei. Both were new countries for me. He also received a card from Cuba for a 20 meter contact on PSK31.
    • He has been playing with packet radio again using a sound card program. Last week he copied several minutes of packet and APRS from the ISS digital repeater.

Jane, KA0USA, worked ON509Q, a Special Event Station in Belgium on 17 meters and received a card from the U.S. Virgin Islands. She also built the Tape Measure Yagi antenna, and she and Bill used it to track the International Space Station on one of its passes last week. She has also been working to catch up on sending out QSL cards.

David, who has changed his call sign to KY6YYY, built a tape measure yagi. He has received the power supply and antenna tuner for his HF rig and is awaiting a microphone and antenna.

Ed, NI5R, recently returned from Arkansas. He worked about 30 CA QSO Party stations and received a QSL card from Russia since the last meeting.

Paul, KB0JIT, stated he had been working on antennas.

Neal, KD0MBL, continues to work DX from his mobile. His latest DX contact was with Germany. He reported working an Alaska station during the Wildlife Refuge Special Event, and has installed a feed through window panel for his home station. He also was one of the members who built a tape measure yagi.

Guest Stan Clark, WB0JRG, is charging new handheld batteries and recently had his generator rebuilt. He brought two commercial direction finding antennas and an attenuator to the meeting.

Program:

Lori, AH6OS, told about a DXpedition she was on to Midway Atoll. Lori, her ham husband, and three other hams went there in December 2001 and January 2002. She reported that since the island is a bird sanctuary by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife, their flights on and off the island had to be made at night. She showed photos taken on Midway, including many WWII historical sites and wildlife including Fairy Terns, Gooney Birds, Frigate Birds and more. The island was beautiful, but the radio room was totally enclosed with concrete and felt claustrophobic since it had no windows. She enjoyed operating the radio, but said she enjoyed touring the island more.
