News From Members:

Kirk, KD0LDH, was one of several members who attended the Megafest swap meet in Monument on Saturday morning. He came home with two "slinky" dipoles for HF. Kirk will be taking his General exam at the August 21 VE session here in Pueblo West.

Neal, KD0MBL, another ham class student, took his General examination at the Megafest VE session and passed it with a perfect score. Congratulations, Neal! You will have fun on the HF bands with that new Yaesu FT-857D.

Ed, NI5R, reported he had not been active except for contacting a few stations in Canada and one in Italy during the last month.

Paul, KB0JIT, has been working with Neal to build an HF fan dipole.

Bill, W0NHF, has been spending time on 10 meters and recently worked TX, FL and the Dakotas.

Bill, KB0RPD, reports he has installed an MFJ window feed through for his antennas. He is pleased with it, and he found he could also raise his "slinky" dipole an additional five feet. He says the antenna seems to work better now on 40 and 20 meters.

Dan, NG0T, notes work has kept him off the air recently, however he worked 8 hours of cw at Field Day and has also been checking into the club's VHF net where shares NCS duties.

Jim, NE0DA, has his 6-meter antenna back in the air and is waiting for another band opening.

Bill, WA0KAQ, stated he had not been on the air much since Field Day, but last Tuesday evening he worked Fiji and Belarus on 20 meter cw running 90 watts to a low dipole.

Jane, KA0USA, was not able to attend, however WA0KAQ says she has received QSL cards from Turks & Caicos Island, the Battleship Missouri, the Galapagos Islands, and a special events card commemorating the 150th Anniversary of the Pony Express. Last Wednesday she also worked a special events station from the Calgary Stampede in Calgary, Alberta.

Old Business:

  • Revision sought on the number of votes required to change the wording of the club's Constitution

    • WA0KAQ stated the former required quorum of 2/3 of the club's voting membership (15 required at the time) was attained by making telephone calls and sending e-mails to members who were not in attendance at the June meeting. Seventeen members cast their votes, and all voted in favor of changing the wording in the Constitution to allow future changes in the document by a 2/3 vote of the licensed members attending the meeting at which the change is requested. Those voting for the change were: Dan, Kevin, Ty, Dwight, Stan, Jim, Jane, Bill E, Ralph, Kirk, Bill F Jr, Bill F III, Andy, Paul, Neal, Bill W & Bill D.

      The New wording is as follows:

        Article VII: Amendments

        a. The Constitution and the listed By-Laws may be amended by a two-thirds vote of the licensed members present at a meeting. Members may propose changes during meetings and such suggestions shall be voted on during the following meeting. Whenever changes are pending, they will be noted in the meeting agenda sent to members so that they may attend the meeting in which action will be taken on the proposals.
         

  • Board of Directors (WA0KAQ & NG0T) - Due to the absence of our treasurer, NG0T, information on the Board of Directors was tabled at the June meeting.

      NG0T reported that Colorado only requires one Director for a non-profit organization. This club has already decided on two Directors.

      Andy, ND0P, and Paul, KB0JIT, were somewhat prematurely elected as Directors at the May 2010 meeting. New Business:

  • Thank you to Pueblo West Rental Center

      Club members signed a Thank You card for Pueblo West Rental Center. The business donated the use of a 5,600-watt gasoline generator for the club's Field Day use. The generator was used to run the air conditioning in the travel trailer.

  • Non-profit Incorporation - Should the club spend $400 to receive non-profit status?
     

      WA0KAQ repeated that Walmart is now taking the position that any organization that is not non-profit is not allowed to sell anything in front of Walmart, effective immediately. This new ruling will prohibit our club from holding future Bake Sales at Walmart unless the organization has non-profit status.

      The club discussed becoming a 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation this year, and was told the filing fee would be about $400. The club has the funds necessary, and if it is going to hold future fundraisers at Walmart, it will need the non-profit status.

      The past two Bake Sales have averaged $250 each in income for our treasury, and Jane would like to have another one in September if the members agree to that. Whether or not the non-profit corporation can be set up by that time, and if the fundraiser can be scheduled early enough, is yet unknown.

      In addition, annual membership dues will start becoming due at the August meeting. The anticipated revenue from those expected to renew their membership by the end of the year is a minimum of $180, and it could be more.

      Membership renewal fees, along with what we would have left in the treasury after the $400 fee was paid, would leave us with an estimated $500 in the treasury at the end of the year. If we can have the fundraiser, it will be more. However, we may be too far into the winter season to hold a Bake Sale when the incorporation is finished.

      KB0RPD made a motion to have NG0T move forward with the non-profit corporation application. The motion was seconded by KB0JIT. The club members voted unanimously to file the necessary paperwork for the non-profit incorporation. NG0T noted it may take some time to get the paperwork together and get the results back. He said a minimum of 90 days would probably be a reasonable time period for the process.

  • Packets for new class members

      Last month it was decided that information packets would be useful if they were given to class members. They would contain membership information and applications, contact information, the website URL, etc. WA0KAQ presented a preliminary packet for members to view. It included a class application form, information about the Technician license, an ARRL handout, a wallet-size card with club contact information, and tri-fold brochure about the club, and a PWARC membership application form.

      In addition, preliminary packets were presented for use by visitors and by new members. All packets will be available soon.

  • Class through Recreation Dept.?

      NE0DA reported that he has been in contact with the Recreation Department about the possibility of using the Liberty Point facility for the club's ham class. He was told that the schedule has already been made for the fall season, but that there was an opening from 8-10 a.m. on Sunday mornings. This time slot was rejected.

      The Department will have a new starting date for its activities in January, and NE0DA was told we might be able to be worked into that schedule.

      No determination was made as to the cost of using the facility, or what it would cost students to take the class there. While it would be nice to have the class information distributed throughout the Metropolitan District through the Recreation Department, it may be cost prohibitive. No decision will be made until all the information can be gathered. We might be better off handling the classes on our own as we have in the past.

  • Field Day results. How did we do?

      NE0DA stated he felt that the dry run held at the June meeting helped considerably in the way things went so smoothly during set up and tear down.

      WA0KAQ praised the members for the way they worked together as a team at Field Day. No matter what the task, they all did their part and helped in any way they could. He also reminded members that several months ago he told them that if they were willing to work at it, their results could surprise some people in the competition as well. He noted that they even surprised him. Although the results won't be known until November, the club did very well for its first event. If this year's score was plugged into last year's results the club would have rated number 7 nationally in a class with 25 stations.

      WA0KAQ stated that now that members have seen how it is done, the club needs to have a Field Day Committee put on the next year's event. All the information WA0KAQ compiled for this year's event will be available to the committee.
