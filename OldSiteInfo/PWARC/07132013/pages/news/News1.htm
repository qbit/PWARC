Since the treasurer, Dan, NG0T, was not in attendance, discussion on whether the club needs a Board of Directors (even if one person), and what legal language should be placed in the Constitution to formally ratify the club's incorporation, was tabled for a future meeting. Auditing the treasury on an annual basis was also discussed, and the consensus of those present was that it was a good idea. This will be on the agenda again at a later date.

NE0DA reported that the club now has a bank account. Only the President, Vice-President and the Treasurer can sign checks, and two signatures are required. All three officers have now signed the bank's signature card. The club had $456 in its treasury at the time of the deposit. The club's incorporation cost $50, and there was a $15 charge for checks from Frontier Bank. He reported that $25 was retained as petty cash as well. That should leave a balance of $366 in the bank account and $25 in petty cash for a total of $391.00 in the treasury.

A discussion was held concerning changing the amount of members needed to constitute a quorum for minor changes to the Constitution that are needed to satisfy incorporation rules. A quorum for this is currently 2/3 of the licensed members, however it is very difficult to get a quorum of members to attend a meeting. It was suggested that perhaps the number of members be lowered for a few upcoming changes in language showing the club is now incorporated. A poll of the members by telephone is an option that can still be used for this. No decision was made, and the item was tabled and will be brought up at a later meeting when the treasurer is present. A statement of dissolution required for incorporation was added to the Constitution last month after a quorum of 2/3 of the membership was contacted (mostly by telephone) and approved the addition. It was decided that the Sunday morning HF net will be discontinued. It will continue to be held on Sunday evenings at 7:30 p.m. on 28.305 MHz following the club's VHF net.

PWARC will offer classes for the Technician Class license on Thursdays beginning January 28. The classes will be held from 7-9:00 p.m. at Fire Station #3, 729 E. Gold Dr. (E. Platteville Blvd/ E. McCulloch Blvd) in north Pueblo West. This is a very nice facility with everything needed for teaching. The classes are free and will be taught by Paul, KB0JIT, and Ed, KD5MHI.

WA0KAQ volunteered and was approved as Trustee for the club's amateur radio license. Following this, President Jim McConnell signed the application for a club call sign. It is hoped the call sign will be issued before the club's next meeting. It will be a sequential call, assigned by the FCC at no charge. If the club wishes, it can trade that call for a Vanity call sign which currently costs $13.40 and is good for 10 years. Since the Trustee is the holder of an Extra Class license, the call sign will be issued as an Extra Class license as well. Should the Trustee change in the future, the license class will change according to the license class of the Trustee, however the call sign will remain the same.

The members approved WA0KAQ's request to continue as the club's webmaster. Bill has been working on a mirror website which can quickly be installed on its own site after the final ham license is received.

Members agreed that an informal gathering for coffee or breakfast and ragchew should be held once a month, approximately half way between meetings. The first Ham Radio Ragchew will be held at 9:00 a.m. Saturday, Feb. 6, at the Hen House Restaurant, 53 Palmer Lake Dr. (cross street, S. Joe Martinez Blvd.). All ham radio operators are invited to attend.

Logistics/Operations Officer, Bill, KB0RPD, announced he has contacted the National Weather Service office in reference to obtaining 12 volt batteries that they are obligated to change on a regular schedule. If the batteries are obtained, they will be distributed to club members who want them. The batteries should make good back up power sources for our radios.

President McConnell appointed Jane, KE6DCE, as head of the club's Ways and Means Committee. Jane will head the club's fundraising activities.

A Volunteer Examiner (VE) session will be held by Pueblo Ham Club from 1-3:00 p.m. Saturday, Feb. 13, at Rawlings Library, 100 E. Abriendo Ave., in Pueblo. Tests for all classes of amateur radio licenses will be available. The cost is $15 (cash, no checks) per test. Further information is on the club's website.

The club continues to look for a Field Day site in Pueblo West, though the community seems to have little to offer. As Field Day is used as a recruiting tool and a way to let residents know about our club, the club is seeking a location that will provide public exposure as well as provide ample space for antennas and other outdoor stations accessories.

KB0RPD reported that Parks and Recreation was not receptive to our use of Lovell Park for Field Day after 10:30 p.m., and the charge would have been $75 per day. That location, which was formerly our first choice, has therefore been rejected.

KB0RPD and NE0DA will meet with a representative of Parks and Recreation on Monday to discuss what could be used in the area of the tennis courts behind the Sheriff's Station. The area has been walked, and we are certain we do not want to use the bare dirt areas or the lightly graveled areas surrounding the tennis courts for parking the motor home or trailer. This area is very soft even when dry, and if there is rain before or during the event, much of this would be a quagmire.

The paved cul-de-sac is an option that has been discussed, as well as the tennis court parking lot. It is doubtful the tennis court parking lot can be obtained however. Parks and Recreation has said they would let the club use this area for much less than they wanted for Lovell Park, and there are porta-potties at the location in the summer months.

Anyone with ideas of a better location in Pueblo West that meets the stated requirements is asked to call Bill, KA0RPD, 547-0694, or Jim, NE0DA, 647-9601.

WA0KAQ gave a program on analog and digital Slow Scan Television (SSTV) using MMSSTV software for analog SSTV and EasyPal software for digital SSTV. Both programs are free and can be downloaded from the Internet. An attempt to connect to an Automatic Packet Reporting System (APRS) through the library's DSL network failed. Bill will try to discover the reason and provide that program at a later date.

The meeting was adjourned at 3:55 p.m.
