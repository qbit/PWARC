Old Business:

1) Change in Constitution

    WA0KAQ requested a revision to change the number of votes needed for changes to the Constitution. The club visited this issue before, but no action was taken. WA0KAQ stated it is getting more difficult to contact a quorum of members It was noted that the club probably only had a quorum of its members at a meeting twice in the history of the club, and this has caused a lot of extra work on the Secretary's part to call and e-mail members each time a change is necessary. The Constitution allows for polling members either by phone or e-mail when issues may be decided.

    More changes will need to be made to the club's Constitution this summer and fall, primarily dealing with detailing with establishing terms of office, duties, etc, for the Board of Directors; and 2) changing the club's corporate status to a non-profit corporate status.

     It was proposed that the required vote of "2/3 of the licensed members" be changed to a vote of "2/3 of the licensed members present at the meeting where changes are discussed." The monthly agenda always references proposed Constitutional changes, allowing members to come to the meeting and voice their opinions.

    A motion was made by Paul, KB0JIT, to change the wording as described above, and this was seconded by Jane, KA0USA. The vote was 10-0 in favor of the change. Under the current wording, however, at least 5 more affirmative votes are needed, and the Secretary will make e-mail contacts, and telephone contacts as necessary to poll the members who were absent.

5. ARRL Affiliation

    The ARRL issued the club a Charter of Affiliation on June 2, 2010. In order to keep this affiliation, the club must retain a 51% ARRL membership.

    One of the benefits allows us to do is to keep $15 of any new ARRL membership fee (which is $39 annually), and $2 of any membership renewal fee, if it is filed through the club. If you are planning to join the ARRL and get QST Magazine, please file through the club. ARRL membership application forms from the secretary. The club can also take credit cards for part of the ARRL membership. Applicants pay the club $15, and ARRL will accept the rest ($24) by credit card if you so desire. If not, the club will accept your check made out to ARRL, and send it to them.

    Webinars

      On April 22 and May 19, WA0KAQ participated in ARRL Public Relations Webinars about how to promote Field Day. They were conducted by the ARRL's Atlantic Division. One of the things stressed was that outside of an actual disaster, Field Day is by far the one day each year that the public gets to see Amateur Radio in action. The presenters also stressed, "You don't get a second chance to make a good first impression." People perceive us as they see us, so it is important that everyone be well-groomed and well dressed for the event. So comb your hair and brush your teeth, and you might want to bring an extra set of clothes along that you can change into after the antennas, shades, and other things are put up on Saturday.

      If someone asks you what Amateur Radio is and what we are doing, each person needs an answer. The presenters suggested that everyone rehearse a 30-second summary of what Amateur Radio is. I don't believe that is necessary, however everyone should think about what he would say if asked. Basically we want to get the point across that:

        1) we are not CBers

        2) we are hobbyists licensed by the FCC after we pass its tests

        3) that we do not rely on any other form of communication such as telephones or the Internet, and

        4) that we are practicing emergency deployment procedures to ensure we are capable of providing back-up communications for any public service agency that requests it should an emergency situation occur.

        5) Amateur Radio is our hobby, and we do not receive compensation for our volunteer work.
