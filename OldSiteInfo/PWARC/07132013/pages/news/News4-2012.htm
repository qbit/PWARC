  • Stan, KC0OYO, has a new Wouxun handheld radio and has installed a 2-meter beam and rotor.

  • Regional Emergency Communications Meeting at Westcliffe

    • Steve Morrisey, Emergency Coordinator for Fremont County held a meeting at the Search and Rescue building in Westcliffe, on March 15. The purposed of the meeting was to attempt to get Amateur Radio/ARES operators to coordinate between the 11 counties in his region to form an emergency communications group. Riley Frazee, a Colorado Regional Emergency Coordinator for the State from Baca County, was also present. Neal (KD0MBL), Steve (KD0QCF), Jeff (KC0CTN), and Bill (KD0RPD) represented our club.

    • Bill, KB0RPD, reported that the main conversation of the meeting centered around connecting all the counties with linked repeaters. Riley Frazee told the group that commercial radio standards are changing, and new standards for the commercial stations now dictate the use of narrow band FM equipment by September 2013. Therefore, commercial radio dealers are beginning to replace good working repeaters with narrow band repeaters. As a result, there will be an abundance of repeaters available at very low prices that could be used for amateur use.

    • Correspondence to the club from Dwight, WB0HBJ, noted there would be expenses involved to change the repeaters for amateur use, as well as maintenance after installation. The repeaters are also somewhat large in size. They are 19-inch rack-mount models the size of a 4' tall file cabinet and weigh 135 lbs.

    • Further meetings will be held on the subject, and Riley Frazee will also be asked to speak about the area's plan to our club within a few months.
