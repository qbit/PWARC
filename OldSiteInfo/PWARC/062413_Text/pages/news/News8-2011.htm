ARRL Membership Report:

The club has 35 members, 34 of whom are licensed. Of those 11 hold Technician licenses, 7 have General licenses, 2 hold Advanced licenses, and 14 have Amateur Extra licenses. Of the 34 voting members, 21 belong to ARRL. (61.8%).

New ARRL Memberships or Renewals: Bob, WB0FBW (New)

News from the members: (What have you done in the last month that pertains to amateur radio?)

  • Ed, NIR5, has his new SignalLink sound card interface set up and is working PSK-31. He reported he had worked about 30 contacts on that mode.

  • Amanda, K1DDN, reported that evening thunderstorms have kept her off the air much of the time and have hampered the 40-meter net she runs.

  • Jim, NE0DA, has been working CW to increase his speed. He noted he had made about a dozen contacts.

  • Dan, NG0T, has not been active during the past month.

  • David, KY6YYY, has been busy on SSB, mostly on 20 meters. He worked 15 states and about as many foreign countries in the past month.

  • Jane, KA0USA, continues to study for her Extra Class license. She now has her own station, a like-new Icom IC-706MKIIG, an Astron RS-20 power supply, and MFJ-949E antenna tuner, and an MFJ-1279M sound card interface for working digital modes. She has been working PSK-31 and has made about a dozen contacts. She also worked a Boy Scout station on Pikes Peak on 20 meter SSB during the Colorado 14er event. Her latest QSL card is from Spain.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, held an APRS mini-workshop on July 30 for K0LDS, KY6YYY, WA6HSI, KB0JIT, KD0OZY and KA0USA. He set up KA0USA's radio and made a chart of antenna tuner settings for the different bands. QSL cards received include one from Macedonia for a RTTY contact and one from Australia for a PSK-31 contact. He reported he had tried the JT65 mode and worked about 6 stations including one in New Zealand. He described the mode as the most boring mode ever devised.

  • Mason, WA6HSI, has his homebrew Slim Jim antenna for 2 meters on the air. He talked to WA0KAQ on the 146.790 repeater at Pueblo.

  • Skip, WB7VXM, recently purchased a new laptop and has made a few contacts on PSK-31.

  • Bill, KB0RPD, reported he had been busy working with the American Red Cross and getting ready for hurricane season.

  • Billy, K5LKT, recently checked in to Army MARS nets in TX and TN.

  • New member Del Milne joined the club this month after talking about ham radio with Gene, K9HBR, and Paul, KB0JIT. All three men are volunteers at the aircraft museum at Pueblo Airport. He notes he is interested in working CW.

Report from Instructors:

Ed, NI5R, reported that the area has two new hams and one upgraded license following the VE Session held before the meeting. The two new technician licenses went to a couple from Canon City, and a ham from the Denver area came to upgrade from Technician to General.

New Business:

  • Run The Rim 5K and 10K Run, Royal Gorge Bridge

    • Royal Gorge Ham Club President Derrick Houska, N0TQ, from Canon City has invited members of our club to participate in the annual Run The Rim event at Royal Gorge Bridge on Saturday, Oct. 1. The race begins on the north side of the bridge at 8:30 a.m. and should be over about 11 a.m. The event will use the 145.490 Royal Gorge Ham Club repeater. The backup simplex frequency is 147.550. We would need to be there about 7:30 a.m.

    • Proceeds from the race go to Starpoint, which serves the communities of Fremont, Chaffee, Custer, Denver and Jefferson Counties. The organization provides services for kids prenatal through five years of age, and to families and adults with cognitive and physical challenges.

    • Any members of the club who would like to help with comms during the event may do so. You will need to have the same equipment as we used in the Liberty Point 5K Run/Walk on July 4. More details will be given as they become available.

  • ARRL School Club Roundup, October 17-21
