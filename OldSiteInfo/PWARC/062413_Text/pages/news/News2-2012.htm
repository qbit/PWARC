News from the members: - (What have you done in the last month that pertains to amateur radio?)

  • Skip, WB7VXM, has a off center fed dipole up at 20' now, but he is having a lot of trouble with S9 noise on 75 meters. He asked for information on antenna tower regulations, and Dan, NG0T, said he would send it to him.

  • Billy, K5LKT, has been spending an hour each day on 15 meters, mostly CW.

  • Mason, WA6HSI, has put together a crystal controlled transmitter. He also talked to a club from La Junta that has five members. He might go visit it next week.

  • Del is studying for his Technician License.

  • Chuck, KC0YMJ, reported no activity.

  • David reported contacts with Australia and Japan.

  • Stanley, WB0JRG, has been monitoring AM stations.

  • Jim, NE0DA, worked Australia on 15 meters and also heard a NY station talking to Dwight, WB0HBJ/m.

  • Amanda, K1DDN, is preparing for an internet show with Bob Heil and Gordon West on twit.com.

  • Dan reported that his rotating dipole fed with open wire line has been damaged by the weather and has been taken down. He plans to refurbish it and offer it to the club for Field Day use. In checking his log recently of the CQ World Wide DX CW Contest, he discovered he had worked 100 countries during the contest. This was his best effort ever.

  • Andy, ND0Q, reported he has been doing many things in the background but has been on the radio little in the last month.

  • Jane, KA0USA, showed a 1st Place plaque her station received in the October Colorado QSO Party.
