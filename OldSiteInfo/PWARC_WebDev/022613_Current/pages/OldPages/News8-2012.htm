News from the members:

  • Steve (KD0QCF) has been studying for his general test. He also passed the test at the VE session.

  • Carrie (KD0SOV) Worked a Missouri station on 10M.

  • Ted (K0LDS) Worked the all Europe contest, worked 16 countries on 15M, and worked IOTA and got several new islands.

  • David (KY6YYY) Worked the VE session and is looking for a new antenna.

  • Dustin (N0DRC) Was activated for the Hurricane Ernesto traffic net. Got his 900 MHZ setup and working.

  • Del (KD0RRH) Got his new handheld programmed.

  • Jim (NE0DA) Did some ant repairs using a long pipe with a nut driver taped to it. Worked a special event station H446MSC in the Solomon Islands to commemorate the Marines landing there in WW2. He worked an aeronautical mobile commercial 737 pilot over Western TN. and worked the Olympics station 2O12L.

  • Bill (WA0KAQ) Worked the VE Session today.
     He Worked a Special Events station in Washington State - 222nd Anniversary of the US Coast Guard.
    Worked a couple of vacationers on top of Pikes Peak. Both were on 2M and were from California.
    Our Scholarship winner, Michelle Oakley contacted him and sent proof she is enrolling in Pueblo Community Collage. Dan sent the scholarship check to the school.
    He ordered and received a new switch for the MFJ memory keyer, but hasn't had time to install it yet.
    Made a couple of coax jumpers and hooked up the high power SWR/Watt meter on the base station.
    Ordered some photo refill pages that will hold club's 4X6 QSL Cards and then put cards in them that the club has received.
    Worked some 6 meter stations during a contest on July 21. Also worked a scout at San Isabel Boy Scout Camp in the mountains west of Rye on 6 meters using a 10 watt PEP rig.
    Worked his first CW station on 6 meters.
    He contacted the Pikes Peak Amateur Radio Association, and they added a link to our club's website from their website.
    He joined a QRP CW group that holds a 90-minute "fox Hunt" on Thursday nights. Two stations are the Foxes, and the hunters are the hounds. He has been able to work 5 of the 6 Foxes so far on 20 meters.
    Bought a "quick release" mount for the Yaesu FT-7800r VHF/UHF rig. Much simpler to use than having to work 4 tiny screws out each time the rig is installed and removed.
    Sent a QSL card to Bolivia.
    He has been playing with a rig he wanted to try for some time and never figured he would get the chance. It's an Icom-756 ProIII.
    He heard the ISS using SSB to talk with a school on July 22.
