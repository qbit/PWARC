(Above) Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club held its second annual ARRL Field Day in the parking lot of Pueblo West Volunteer Fire Department Station No. 1. (Below right) Ed (NI5R) and Lindon (KC6ELM) at back, Jane (KA0USA), Dan (NG0T) and Paul KB0JIT) helped keep the bands busy.

Fifteen PWARC members and one guest operator signed in at the information table for the 2011 ARRL Field Day Event on June 25 and 26 in Pueblo West.

Those signing in over the weekend were Paul Sipes (KB0JIT), Dan Broadbooks (NG0T), Andy Lloyd (ND0P), Neal Tew (KD0MBL), Jane Ewing (KA0USA), Lori Miner (AH6OS), Ed Force (NI5R), Bill Ewing (WA0KAQ), Jim McConnell (NE0DA), David Yeater (KY6YYY), Bill Fortune (W0NHF), Stan Dorris (KC0OYO), newly licensed member Clark Clendenen (KD0OZY), two new members Lindon Ungerman (KC6ELM) and Edwin "Skip" Richards (WB7VXM), and guest operator David Ewing (KC0RNI) of Westminster.

Those who participated in operating radios or logging were Paul Sipes (KB0JIT), Dan Broadbooks (NG0T), Andy Lloyd (ND0P), Neal Tew (KD0MBL), Jane Ewing (KA0USA), Bill Ewing (WA0KAQ), Ed Force (NI5R), Jim McConnell (NE0DA), David Yeater (KY6YYY), and David Ewing (KC0RNI).

This year's accomodations included two pop-up shades for operators and a tent for storage. Unlike last year when the club lost a few hours of operation due to lighting in the area, no storms visited our site this year. A breeze helped cool the operating postions as the thermometer hovered in the 90s F.

The club again ran QRP at 5 watts on all the radios, with power supplied only by batteries charged by solar panels. Neal (KD0MBL) mounted two 120-watt panels on the back of his vehicle, and Ed (NI5R) brought a 45-watt array.

Radios supplied by NE0DA (Icom IC-706), KB0JIT (Yaesu FT-857D), and KD0MBL (Yeasu FT-857D) handled most of the work. Antennas included a Butternut vertical supplied by WA0KAQ and KB0JIT, a G5RV from NE0DA, and a Tarheel mobile antenna mounted on KD0MBL's vehicle. All seemed to work well, except for one instance. The telescoping support for the G5RV did not stay locked and dropped to about 8' at the center. KB0JIT was wondering why he wasn't making any contacts for a while. After his unintended "Near Vertical Incidence Skywave" (NVIS) antenna was discovered, and the problem was quickly remedied with help from KC0RNI and WA0KAQ.

KB0JIT was especially happy with the results received from the Butternut vertical he had spend so much time and energy readying for the event. It was used on the CW station, and NG0T, broke his 2010 record of 119 CW contacts by making 129 contacts this year.

New member Lindon Ungerman, KC6ELM, of Westcliffe (below) was excited to be a part of our 2011 Field Day team. Lindon passed his General examination at our June 18 VE Session. In addition to helping with SSB contacts, Lindon also set up a camping tent which was used for storage.

Thanks to efforts of KA6YYY, the event received front page coverage from The Pueblo Chieftain newspaper on Sunday, June 26. The Chieftain sent photographer Mike Sweeney and reporter Adrian Garcia to cover the event. Dispite the mistakes of calling Field Day a "Hamfest" and saying the event was sponsored by "the National Association for Amateur Radio" the two did well.

Sweeney seemed quite interested in the hobby and stayed to see if we would be lucky enough to communicate with someone through a satellite. KC0RNI and WA0KAQ were unsuccessful at this effort, hearing the satellite only for a few seconds. They will be putting more effort into this project and with more experience hope to be successful next year. Sweeney interviewed WA0KAQ (below), and a photo showing him and his son KC0RNI trying to communicate with the satellite appeared in the newspaper. A photo of NG0T operating CW also accompanied the article.

An article written by WA0KAQ also appeared on Thursday before the event in the Pueblo West View.
