WA0KAQ Selected Grand Marshall Of
2012 Fourth of July Parade

Photo above by David Yeater, KY6YYY.

Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club founders Bill Ewing (WA0KAQ) and his wife Jane (KA0USA) had a unique experience on the Fourth of July. Bill was selected as Grand Marshall of the 2012 parade, and the couple traveled the parade route in a modified buckboard.

Following the parade, Bill also judged the entries.

After learning of the honor at the PWARC's Field Day site on June 23, ARRL Assistant Section Manager Ben Baker, KB0UBZ, from Denver commented that in all the years he has been a ham he has never known of another ham ever being the Grand Marshall of a parade.

WA0KAQ Recognized For Contributions To Amateur Radio and Emergency Communications Work

On May 22, 2012 Bill Ewing, WA0KAQ, was recognized for his work in the amateur radio hobby and for many years of dedicated service in Emergency Communications.

Bill, an amateur radio operator for more than 48 years, was surprised at the Pueblo West Metropolitan District's evening Board meeting where the awards were presented. He thought he was there in reference to a Proclamation for the Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club during the upcoming ARRL Field Day.

The Board presented Bill with a Proclamation naming June 23 & 24 as "Bill Ewing Days" in Pueblo West. That weekend also coincides with this year's ARRL Field Day exercise.

The Pueblo County Sheriff's Department also presented him with a plaque for his many years of service to the Amateur Radio Emergency Service (ARES).

While a representative was not present that evening, the Pueblo West Fire Department later presented Bill with a certificate of appreciation. Photo by KY6YYY

PWARC Celebrates National Library Month For Third Year

President Jane Ewing (left) presented a Certificate of Recognition to Debra Krauth of Pueblo West Library at the club's April 14, 2012 meeting. The certificate was in recogition of National Library Month and the service and facilities the library has provided to our club.

An annual subscription to CQ Magazine was also renewed for the library.

May 14, 2011
Fox Hunt

Three intrepid fox hunters from our club showed up for the May 14 Fox Hunt. Club President Jane Ewing (KA0USA), Vice-president David Yeater (KY6YYY), and Instructor Paul Sipes (KB0JIT) spent the afternoon outwitting and being outwitted by the two foxes (hidden transmitters).

The foxes were hidden by Fox Hunt Coordinator Neal Tew (KD0MBL) and club Secretary Bill Ewing (WA0KAQ).

Club Recognizes National Library Month
April 16, 2011

In 2010, Pueblo West Amaterur Radio Club presented Pueblo West Library with a subscription to CQ Magazine, a magazine pertaining to the amateur radio hobby, in honor of National Library Month. This year the club decided to renew the subscription for another year in honor of the same occasion.

Club President Jane Ewing, KA0USA, (left) presented Ms. Shimizo with a Certificate of Recognition at the club's April 16 meeting.

Jane expressed the club's appreciation for the use of the fine facility the library has provided us for the last 18 months, and for the fine service we have received from the library staff.

Ms. Shimizo replied that the library was very happy to have our club using its facility.

First sponsored in 1958, National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association and libraries across the country.
