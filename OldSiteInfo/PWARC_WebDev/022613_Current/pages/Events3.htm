Liberty Point 5K Run - July 4, 2012

United Way Representative Bianca Hicks (L) and Race Director Stan Herns welcomed the help from the ham radio community as course monitors. Lori, AH60S, (R) was stationed at the start/finish line.

PWARC members KB0JIT, NE0DA, KD0QCF, volunteer N0DRC, N0OBV, and KD0QMS helped provide communications for the 5th Annual Liberty Point 5K Run. Not shown are KY6YYY and AH6OS.
Photo by KY6YYY

This year's event hosted 516 runners and walkers from around Southern Colorado and even other parts of the nation. Proceeds from the event go to the United Way of Pueblo County. Last year, programs funded by the United Way helped more than 11,000 people in Pueblo County.

2012 Bake Sale
June 3, 2012

Open For Business!

Pueblo West ARC Ways and Means volunteer Jane Ewing (KA0USA) and Sharon Fortune had the goodies spread out and ready for the first customer at 8:00 a.m. Sunday, June 3 in front of the Walmart Super Center in Pueblo West.

The sale brought the club its most successful venture to date by bringing in $563. Not included in that total are a few promised donations yet to be collected. This amount is almost double that collected at the club's first Bake Sale in 2010.

The first $250 in proceeds from the sale go toward the PWARC scholarship fund for a Pueblo West High School graduating senior in 2013. The balance of the funds will go in the club's General Fund. ( Photo by KE0FI)

Armed Forces Day
May 19, 2012

Design by Bill Ewing, WA0KAQ

Pueblo West Amateur Radio Club held a Special Event Station at Weisborg B-24 Aircraft Museum at Pueblo Airport on Saturday, May 19 to honor our nation's Armed Forces. The museum also held its Second Annual Fly-In that day. Jim, NE0DA, and Stan, KC0OYO, (above right) were among the club's members who attended.

The station consisted of a Yaesu FT-101E transceiver from the early 1980's and an all-band dipole. Operators made their contacts from beside a WW-II B-29 bomber.

Band conditions were poor during the morning hours, but in the afternoon 20 meters came alive. The operators found themselves to be the focal point of a pile-up of stations wanting to work them. According to the club's QSL Manager, K0LDS, a total of 82 contacts were made.
