ARRL Membership Report:

  • The club has 32 members, 31 of whom are licensed. Of those 8 hold Technician licenses, 7 have General Licenses, 2 hold Advanced licenses, and 15 have Amateur Extra licenses. Of the 32 voting members, 23 belong to ARRL. (71.19%).

New ARRL Memberships or Renewals: None

News from the members: (What have you done in the last month pertaining to amateur radio?)

  • Bill, KD0RPD, has been working with a computer program to control his rig.

  • Andy, ND0P, is building a screwdriver antenna.

  • Neal, KD0MBL, has been very active on 2 meters and is refurbishing a VHF/UHF beam. He wants to learn how to use Packet on 2 meters.

  • Bill, W0NHF, has been working the HF bands.

  • Mason, WA6HSI, has been in contact with the astronomy club at CSU Pueblo and is interested in using their radio telescope on 23 cm.

  • Dwight, WB0HBJ, reported he had worked stations in Pueblo West while being on the road, as well as making a mobile to mobile contact with a station in Australia on 40 meters. He is also experimenting with digital voice radio using D-Star.

  • Stan, KC0OYO, has been working stations on 10 and 40 meters. He has also worked Dwight on 60 and 160 meters.

  • Amanda, K1DDN, reports she has been involved in her regular 40 meter net. She is also part of a weekly internet ham radio program with Bob Heil and Gordon West. She said husband Jeff, K0JSC, recently acquired a Motorola turbo digital repeater and is getting it set up.

  • Paul, KB0JIT, drove 8,000 miles in the last 1-1/2 months and has not been on the radio much. While on vacation, he had eyeballs with two hams in Georgia.

  • Jim, NE0DA, reported that the wind damaged his HF beam. He has ordered a replacement part for it, but the part is on back order from Cushcraft.

  • Jane, KA0USA, has been busy helping her husband Bill, WA0KAQ, recuperate from his stay in the hospital and has only been on the club's weekly net.

  • Bill, WA0KAQ, has only been on two meters a few times in the last month. He did make one HF contact. He worked Namibia in Africa for a new country contact.

  • Steve, KD0QCF, has been active working club members on two meters. He also made several 10 meter contacts last weekend.

Old Business:

  • Scholarship

    • Only one application was received. It was given to the club's Board of Directors. The Board will decide whether the candidate meets the requirements set by the club.

Special Event Station

  • The club will hold a Special Event on May 19 during the 2nd Annual Weisbrod B-24 Aircraft Museum Fly-in. The museum is located at 31001 Magnuson Ave. near Pueblo Airport. There is a Yaesu FT-101E transceiver at the site, however Neal, KD0MBL, will have a portable station on standby due to the age of the equipment. Operators will be needed from 9 a.m. to 4 p.m.

  • We need to take photos at the event to be made into QSL cards.

  • WA0KAQ will send the information to the ARRL, but it will probably be too late to have it published.

New Business:

  • Field Day

    • Field Day Committee members have received the 2011 Field Day After Action Report and will soon start planning for this year's event to be held June 23 & 24. Some planning has already been done.

  • Regional Emergency Communications Meeting

    • Steve Morrisey, Emergency Coordinator for Fremont County will hold a meeting at the Search and Rescue building in Westcliffe, on March 15 at 6pm. Morrisey is attempting to get Amateur Radio/ARES operators to coordinate between the 11 counties in his region to form an emergency communications group. Riley Frasie from Baca County a Colorado Regional Emergency Coordinator for the State, will also be present. Neal (KD0MBL), Steve (KD0QCF), Jeff (KC0CTN), and Bill (KD0RPD) plan to attend the meeting.

  • Donation for National Library Week in April

    • The club will again donate an annual subscription of CQ Magazine to Pueblo West Library in April in commemoration of National Library Month. Bill, KB0RPD, made the motion to purchase the subscription, and Jim, NE0DA, seconded the motion. The motion was unanimously approved. WA0KAQ will make the renewal.

  • Emergency planning

    • Neal (KD0MBL) and Steve (KD0QCF) will check with the hospital in Pueblo West to access their emergency communications needs.
